# Understanding Backlinks

Backlinks are the foundation of Nalanda's knowledge graph. They create automatic connections between your content.

## How Backlinks Work

When you write `[[concept name]]` in your content, Nalanda:

1. **Parses** the reference during save/publish
2. **Creates** a concept page if it doesn't exist
3. **Links** your post to that concept
4. **Displays** your post as a backlink on the concept page

## Example

```markdown
This post discusses [[Machine Learning]] and its applications in [[Natural Language Processing]].

Related techniques include [[Deep Learning]] and [[Neural Networks]].
```

This creates:
- 4 concept pages (if they don't exist)
- 4 connections from this post to those concepts
- 4 backlinks visible on each concept page

## Benefits

### Discoverability

Readers can easily navigate from one concept to related content.

### Knowledge Emergence

Over time, patterns emerge showing which concepts are central to your thinking.

### Non-linear Reading

Readers can explore your knowledge base in any order, following connections that interest them.

## Best Practices

1. **Be consistent** with concept naming
2. **Link liberally** - more connections create a richer graph
3. **Use specific concepts** rather than generic terms
